# Our Philosophy

*"You can't win if you don't play. So play." -- someone*

Our investment philosophy has been developed over an entire career of research, analysis, and experience. We regard our investment philosophy as a living document - one which reflects our learnings over time.

As of writing, we would triangulate our philosophy using the following notes:

1. **We make big calls.** We care about the long-term trajectory of our client's investments. As a result, we avoid predicting the next quarter's earnings or analyzing the thoughts and speeches of Presidents. We believe Apple's Q2 1993 Diluted EPS and President Eisenhower's 1954 State of the Union address have been ultimately rendered immaterial to the history returns in the stock market. We worry about the big things and ignore the small things.

2. **We care what we pay.** Every investment is riskless at a low enough price and infinitely risky at a high enough price. If you gifted us common shares for free, we'd make an instantaneous profit by flipping it into the market. We view our job as figuring out the threshold price at which risk becomes negligible, and doing our best to buy at prices below that threshold.

3. **Following the crowd can be lucrative.** We don't need to be contrarian to make money. We just need to be right. Whether we are with or against the crowd does not matter to us. What matters is that our clients end up better off because of the decisions we make.

4. **Flexibility above all.** Underwriting risk is not about knowing everything - it's about knowing enough to make a decision. We believe it's better to find comfort in uncertainty and retain the flexibility to change course when the environment demands.

5. **We pick securities.** We know how to buy stocks, bonds, commodities, currencies, and assets. We don't know how to buy "Quality" (factors) or "Healthcare" (sectors). Whatever factors we find ourselves exposed to tend to be pure consequence of bottoms-up construction.

6. **We never compromise on integrity.** Management with decades of experience can still mismanage a great business. We care what managers choose to tell us and what we have to find out on our own. We will ruthlessly scrutinize the integrity of managers, because we have no choice but to rely on them.

7. **Some things are too hard for us.** We are perfectly happy to "miss out" on potentially lucrative investment opportunities, if we do not understand how to price them. We think the investable universe is plentiful enough for us to find our fill elsewhere.

8. **We reject probabilistic thinking.** When tomorrow arrives, a stock cannot be 60% up and 40% down. We believe planning for such an eventuality does not reflect reality.
