# Our Philosophy

*"You can't win if you don't play. So play." -- someone*

We regard our investment philosophy as a living document - one which reflects our learnings over time.

As of writing, we would triangulate our philosophy using the following notes:

1. **We make big calls.** Time and again, we encounter investors who insist on predicting the next quarter's earnings or analyzing the thoughts and speeches of Presidents. While we could pore over Apple's Q2 1993 Diluted EPS or the contents of and reactions to President Eisenhower's 1954 State of the Union address, history seems to have rendered those events (and many others) completely immaterial to long-term returns. We worry about the big things and ignore the small things.

2. **We care what we pay.** Every investment is riskless at a low enough price and infinitely risky at a high enough price. If you gifted us common shares for free, we'd make an instantaneous profit by flipping it into the market. We view our job as figuring out the threshold price at which risk becomes negligible, and doing our best to buy at prices below that threshold.

3. **Following the crowd can be lucrative.** We don't need to be contrarian to make money. We just need to be right. Whether we are with or against the crowd does not matter to us. What matters is that our clients end up better off because of the decisions we make.

4. **Flexibility above all.** Underwriting risk is not about knowing everything - it's about knowing enough to make a decision. In our experience, those who know everything are either 1) being dishonest or 2) wasting time. We believe it's better to find comfort in uncertainty and retain the flexibility to change course when the environment demands.

5. **We pick securities.** Some investors think in terms of sectors, factors, or themes, but we still don't know how to buy "Low Volatility". We buy stocks, bonds, commodities, currencies, etc. Whatever factors we find ourselves exposed to tend to purely be a consequence of bottoms-up construction.

6. **We never compromise on integrity.** We're not concerned with where company management went to school or how many hundreds of years of combined experience they have. We care what they choose to tell us and what we have to find out on our own. We are not transparency maximalists - as we understand there are many valid reasons for discretion - but we will ruthlessly scrutinize the integrity of managers, because we have no choice but to rely on them.

7. **Some things are too hard for us.** We are perfectly happy to "miss out" on potentially lucrative investment opportunities, if we do not understand how to price them. We think we owe our clients that duty and that the investable universe is plentiful enough for us to find more than our fill elsewhere.
